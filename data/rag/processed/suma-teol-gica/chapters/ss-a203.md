# Segunda Parte da Segunda Parte (SS)
## Capítulo 203: whether we were never guilty of his sin; and then we must remember that

we are men, and might have been guilty of it; or that we once had it on
our conscience, but have it no longer: and then we should bethink
ourselves that we are all weak, in order that our reproof may be the
outcome, not of hatred, but of pity. But if we find that we are guilty
of the same sin, we must not rebuke him, but groan with him, and invite
him to repent with us." It follows from this that, if a sinner reprove
a wrongdoer with humility, he does not sin, nor does he bring a further
condemnation on himself, although thereby he proves himself deserving
of condemnation, either in his brother's or in his own conscience, on
account of his previous sin.
Hence the Replies to the Objections are clear.
